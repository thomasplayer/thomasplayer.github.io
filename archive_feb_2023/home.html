Thomas Player

Science writer and tutor in Oxford. Interested in anything creative.

I recently finished my PhD (DPhil) at the University of Oxford where I worked in the Department of Chemistry. My research as part of the Hore group focused on using theory and computation to model systems of potential biological significance, with projects including:

  • Quantum cognition? Assessing the feasibility of a recent theory of cognition by calculating relaxation times for small calcium phosphate clusters.
  • Magnetoreception: investigating the reactions responsible for magnetic sensing and navigation in species such as migratory birds.
  • Magnetic effects on chemical reactions: modelling the effect of magnetic fields on a reaction of CO2.

Alongside science, I am interested in writing, publishing, education, and anything creative.
